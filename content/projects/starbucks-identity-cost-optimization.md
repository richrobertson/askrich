## Recruiter Summary

Led backend work that replaced a third-party identity provider with a custom-built SAML token generator, reducing annual software licensing costs by $200K while preserving critical enrollment flows.

## Problem

Starbucks relied on a third-party identity provider in flows tied to Starbucks credit and prepaid card enrollment. The dependency carried ongoing licensing cost and created an opportunity for a more controlled internal implementation.

## Scope

- Identity-related backend flow redesign
- SAML token generation for partner redirect flows
- Cost reduction without disrupting customer enrollment behavior

## My Role

As Engineer Lead, I was responsible for architecture and backend delivery decisions across multiple customer-facing service teams and helped lead the work that replaced the external provider.

## Key Design Decisions

- Replaced the third-party identity provider with a custom-built SAML token generator.
- Kept the implementation aligned with existing customer-facing enrollment flows so cost savings did not come at the expense of user experience.
- Treated the effort as both a platform simplification and a business-cost optimization initiative.

## Technologies

SAML, backend services, partner integration flows, customer account and enrollment systems.

## Outcomes

- Reduced software licensing costs by $200K annually.
- Preserved key customer enrollment flows for Starbucks credit and prepaid cards.
- Reduced dependency on an external identity provider for a business-critical workflow.

## Leadership Signals

- Combined business cost reduction with technical simplification.
- Applied backend architecture judgment to a customer-facing identity integration problem.
- Helped align engineering work with measurable business value.
