## 10) Tell me about a failure or setback.

Strategic answer:
Not every modernization step lands cleanly on the first attempt. In setbacks, I focus on quick stabilization, root-cause clarity, and explicit prevention steps. The key lesson is to make feedback loops shorter: stronger validation gates, clearer ownership, and earlier risk surfacing. Setbacks are expensive only when teams fail to encode the learning into the delivery process.

## 18) Tell me about a time you handled a high-pressure incident.

Strategic answer:
In high-pressure situations, I focus the team on three steps: stabilize, diagnose, and communicate. Stabilization protects users, diagnosis prevents repeated mistakes, and communication aligns stakeholders on expected recovery path. This disciplined sequence prevents panic-driven thrash and speeds real recovery. I have used this approach effectively across production-facing systems.

## Optional Follow-Up Angles

- Decision framework: "I optimize for reliability risk, reversibility, and long-term change velocity."
- Collaboration signal: "I make tradeoffs explicit early so teams can align before implementation diverges."
- Ownership signal: "I stay accountable through rollout and post-deploy outcomes, not only feature completion."
- Mentoring signal: "I invest in repeatable engineering habits so quality scales beyond one project."
