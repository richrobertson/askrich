## 4) Tell me about a time you reduced risk in production.

Strategic answer:
During OCI migration work, production risk was reduced by combining staged traffic control patterns with stronger observability. Instead of treating migration as a one-way switch, we treated it as a monitored sequence with rollback-aware checkpoints. The practical impact was fewer unknowns during rollout and a safer path to adoption. I focus on making reliability an explicit design deliverable, not a post-launch cleanup task.

## 5) Describe a time you improved a legacy system.

Strategic answer:
In Oracle RQS modernization, the challenge was accumulated technical debt that made secure upgrades and change management slower than needed. I helped drive onboarding to stronger dependency management and refactored service-client behavior into common, testable providers. This directly reduced security compliance burden and improved maintainability. Legacy systems improve fastest when you pair tactical delivery with selective architecture corrections.

## 8) Tell me about a time you had to move quickly.

Strategic answer:
The Java 8 to Java 17 modernization and AMD to ARM transition for Oracle RQS across 32 data centers had a compressed timeline. We kept speed by narrowing scope to high-leverage work, using disciplined rollout sequencing, and focusing the team on blocker removal over perfectionism. The effort completed in under three months. Fast execution worked because the plan protected reliability-critical paths.

## 11) Describe a time you improved system reliability.

Strategic answer:
Reliability improvements in my work usually come from architecture plus operations together: safer rollout patterns, tighter observability, and simplifying fragile integration points. In cloud migration work, this approach reduced incident risk during change windows and made service behavior easier to reason about. Reliability is often improved most by reducing complexity around critical paths.

## 12) Tell me about a time you drove measurable business impact.

Strategic answer:
At Starbucks, one direct business result was reducing software licensing costs by about $200K annually by replacing a third-party identity component with a fit-for-purpose SAML token generator for a key onboarding flow. At Oracle, migration and modernization work contributed to strategic platform outcomes tied to large business opportunities. I try to make impact legible in both technical and business terms.
